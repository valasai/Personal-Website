Teaching

Engineering Education and Student Mentorship

Teaching Philosophy

I believe that effective teaching goes beyond delivering course content. My approach focuses on creating an engaging learning environment where students can develop critical thinking skills, practical problem-solving abilities, and a deep understanding of engineering principles. I emphasize the connection between theoretical knowledge and real-world applications, particularly in the context of Pakistan's engineering challenges.

My teaching philosophy centers on student-centered learning, where I act as a facilitator rather than just a lecturer. I encourage active participation, collaborative learning, and hands-on experiences that help students understand complex engineering concepts. I also integrate current research findings and industry practices into my courses to keep the curriculum relevant and up-to-date.

I am committed to mentoring students not only in their academic pursuits but also in their personal and professional development. Through my role as a thesis supervisor and mentor, I guide students in developing research skills, critical thinking, and professional ethics that will serve them throughout their careers in engineering.
