Research Overview

My research focuses on developing integrated modeling frameworks for sustainable energy transitions, with particular emphasis on the intersection of climate, energy, water, and food systems (CLEWS). I employ advanced optimization techniques, artificial intelligence, and system dynamics modeling to address critical challenges in energy planning, renewable energy integration, green hydrogen economics, and energy equity.

My work spans multiple scales—from community-level microgrids to national energy systems and international energy corridors. I collaborate with institutions across Pakistan, Turkey, Australia, Saudi Arabia, the United States, and Europe, maintaining active research profiles on ResearchGate with over 110,615 reads and 1,654 citations. The ultimate goal is to provide evidence-based, implementable solutions that enable developing and developed nations to achieve climate commitments while ensuring energy security, economic development, and social equity.

Research Focus Areas

Green Hydrogen Economics and Trade

Development of comprehensive models for hydrogen production, storage, transportation, and international trade corridors, including techno-economic analysis and policy frameworks for hydrogen deployment.

Tools: OSeMOSYS, Pyomo, Julia/JuMP, Gurobi, GLPK

Artificial Intelligence in Energy Systems

Application of machine learning, deep learning, and AI-driven optimization to improve energy forecasting, market modeling, and system operations.

Tools: TensorFlow, PyTorch, scikit-learn, XGBoost, pandas, NumPy

Water-Energy-Food Nexus Modeling

Integrated system dynamics and optimization models addressing the complex interdependencies between water, energy, and food security.

Tools: Pyomo, Gurobi, System Dynamics, TIMES framework

National and Regional Energy System Optimization

Long-term capacity expansion and dispatch optimization models for national energy systems, including PakistanTIMES 2025 and international energy corridor analysis.

Tools: AnswerTIMES (MARKAL), OSeMOSYS, Pyomo, LEAP Energy Model

Current Research Projects

Open-Source Julia Framework for Multi-Regional Hydrogen Trade and Infrastructure Optimization

Active
Internal 2024-2025 Pakistan-Middle East Corridor

Development of the first fully open-source, Julia-based optimization framework for multi-regional hydrogen trade analysis. This project addresses the critical research gap in transparent, reproducible hydrogen trade modeling by creating a comprehensive framework that explicitly models all major hydrogen carriers (H₂, NH₃, LOHC, LH₂) with conversion losses and detailed infrastructure investment decisions.

Key Objectives:

  • Develop first fully transparent hydrogen trade optimization framework using Julia/JuMP
  • Model multi-carrier hydrogen trade (compressed H₂, ammonia, LOHC, liquid H₂)
  • Optimize infrastructure investments for Pakistan-Middle East hydrogen corridor
  • Create open-source code repository for community use and reproducibility
  • Analyze policy scenarios including carbon pricing and renewable mandates
Expected Impact: First open-source framework enabling reproducible hydrogen policy analysis, targeting publication in Applied Energy (IF: 10.1)
